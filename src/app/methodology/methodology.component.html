Methodology

The data index considers a collection of research areas that broadly stroke an idea of the level of completeness in the knowledge of a given period and timeframe. These areas are:

  • Agriculture - These datasets and researches regard the area of agriculture, such as for example idetification of agricultural land use or of crops cultivated in the past in specific areas
  • Climate - These datasets and researches cover the changes in climate in the past.
  • Economy - These datasets and researches regard the changes in economy and the possible normalizations of the past with modern economic conditions.
  • Entertainment - These datasets and researches regard the world of entertainment, from circus to theatre to cinema
  • Industry - These datasets and researches cover the changes im the industrial complex of the past.
  • Infrastructures - roads - These datasets and researches regard the road infrastructure and its evolution.
  • Infrastructures - water - These datasets and researches regard the water based infrastructure and its evolution.
  • Infrastructures - air - These datasets and researches regard the airway infrastructure and its evolution.
  • Politics - These datasets and researches regard the changes in borders
  • Religion - These datasets and researches cover the distribution and acrivities related to religion
  • Urban - These datasets and researches regard urban evolution over time.
  • Ephemeral - These datasets and researches regard movements of people, ships and anythin mobile
  • War - These datasets and researches regard detailed aspects of war.
  • Geography - These datasets and researches regard the changes in nature, both totally natural and antropic
All of these areas have sub-topics to expand the aspects of the main topic they analyze. These sub-topics are:
  • Location - These researches and datasets give us the point localization of analyzed items.
  • Structure - This sub-topic regards researches that resulted in the creation of open datasets of the planimetry of buildings, cities, areas.
  • Model - This sub-topic regards activities that resulted in the creation of 3D models for specific buildings or items of the main topic.
  • Events - These datasets and researches give us timelines (as datasets or not) about the specific topic in the specific analyzed period
  • Usage - These datasets and researches give us context and modes for the fruition of the
  • Indexes - These datasets and researches analyze specific indicators, such as for example salary over time or salary in ancient Rome normalized or GDP normalized to the past.
  • General - These datasets and researches researches cover the main topic in general or without a specific viewpoint.

In addition to this main categorization, we are using OHM related indicators, such as Source Reliability and Source Precision, both indicators that span from 1 to 6 being 1 the least precise or reliable, and 6 the most precise or reliable in order to be able to classify the quality of the datasets.

Specifically the reliability is divided as follows

  • 6 - Academic peer-reviewed research / Excavation Report
  • 5 - In-period source material
  • 4 - Review papers
  • 3 - Non peer-reviewed research
  • 2 - Local public history activities
  • 1 - Hearsay

Similarly the source quality is divided into 6 categories

  • 6 - Precise dataset with well defined methodologies and tools
  • 5 - ...
  • 4 - ...
  • 3 - ...
  • 2 - ...
  • 1 - Low quality dataset, with visible discrepancies and/or errors
